## Design Context

### Users
Both casual puzzle solvers and serious Sudoku enthusiasts. Casual users want a relaxing, distraction-free solving experience. Serious solvers want to learn advanced strategies (X-Wing, Swordfish, Forcing Chains, etc.) and improve their logic skills. Used on both phones (one-handed, on the go) and tablets (relaxed, longer sessions).

### Brand Personality
Calm, bookish, analog. Like solving on paper — warm, quiet, tactile. The app is "a quiet space for focus and clarity." Think: a well-worn newspaper, a Kindle, a library reading room.

### Aesthetic Direction
- Serif italic typography evoking print/paper
- Warm, muted color palettes (default "Kindle Paper" theme)
- 8 themes ranging from warm paper to code-editor-inspired dark modes
- Minimal chrome, generous spacing, no visual noise
- Anti-references: loud game apps, neon-accented dark UIs, gamified puzzle apps with coins/streaks/ads

### Design Principles
1. **Focus first** — Nothing on screen that doesn't serve the puzzle
2. **Paper heritage** — Evoke the tactile, analog feel of pencil-on-paper solving
3. **Depth without complexity** — Approachable surface, powerful strategies underneath
4. **Quiet confidence** — The UI should feel assured and understated, never flashy
5. **Teach through doing** — Hints explain the logic, not just the answer
